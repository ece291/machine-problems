High Resolution Graphics with VESA

320x200x256 was nice.  You needed a pallette, so that was kind of icky.   If you ever opened up one of the .bmp files from MP4 in a windows image editor, you saw how small they really were.  You want cool graphics, these days you use a high resolution, full color mode.  That's what this section is all about.  There is an excellent introduction to this stuff at http://www.osha.igs.net/~dandelong/nw/index.htm under PC Graphics / SVGA.  He's got stuff on Bresenham's Algorithm too!  Spend at least ten minutes looking over this.  Of course you won't look over it unless I ask you a Question 5:  How would you set the Set VBE Video Mode to 1024*768*24bit color so that we could access the screen using a linear array?  Give me numbers!
