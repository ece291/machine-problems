Multiplicative Inverse Modulo X (a quick and dirty definition)

First you need to understand what modulo (mod) is.  The modulus simply the remainder of a division of two integers.  For example, if you divide 11 by 2, you get the quotient of 5 with a remainder of 1.  Put in terms of modulus, 11 mod 2 = 1.  With modulus the quotient of the division doesn't really matter, just the remainder..

In algebra every number has an inverse.  For example, the inverse of 2 is 1/2 or the inverse of 1/5 is 5.  Another way of defining an inverses could be that a number multiplied by its inverse is equal to 1.  For example 2*1/2 = 1 or 1/5*5.

Suppose you had the following (A*B) mod X = 1,  in this equation A and B would be multiplicative inverses modulo X of each other.   For example  (2*5) mod 9 = 1,  2 is the multiplicative inverses modulo 9 of 5,  and 5 is the multiplicative inverses modulo 9 of 2.  Just to drive in the point..

3*7 = 21   and  21 mod 5 = 1   so  (3*7) mod 5 = 1   therefore 3 and  7 are multiplicative inverses modulo 5 of each other.
